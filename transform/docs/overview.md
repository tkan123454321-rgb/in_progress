# 🚀 Lakehouse Data Platform Architecture

Welcome to the Data Warehouse documentation. Our pipeline follows a modern Medallion Architecture to ensure data quality, traceability, and performance.

---

## 🥉 1. Staging Layer (Raw & Standardized)
The Staging layer serves as the **Technical Standardization** zone. The primary goal here is to clean the raw data formats without applying any business or filtering logic. The data grain remains exactly the same as the Bronze layer.

**Key Transformation Steps:**
1. **Naming Conventions:** Standardize all column names to `snake_case`, removing any leading/trailing whitespaces and special characters.
2. **Type Casting:** Convert raw string values into appropriate data types.
3. **JSON Parsing (if applicable):** Extract nested properties from raw JSON strings into dedicated physical columns.
4. **Audit Columns:** Inject metadata columns generated by dbt to allow data lineage tracking.

*Note: No data rows are filtered (dropped) in this layer. Bad data is passed through to be caught and handled in the Silver layer.*

---

## 🥈 2. Silver Layer (Cleansed & Conformed)
The Silver layer serves as the **Data Cleansing and Conformed** zone. While the Staging layer focuses on technical standardization, the primary goal of the Silver layer is to enforce business rules, ensure data integrity, and reshape the data into a clean, consumable format for downstream analytics.

**Key Transformation Steps:**
1. **Deduplication:** Remove exact duplicate records or keep the most recent record based on primary keys and update timestamps.
2. **Data Quality Validation:** Apply rigorous data quality checks. Records that violate business logic are flagged as `unqualified` or filtered out.
3. **Data Reshaping (if applicable):** Perform operations such as pivoting, unpivoting, or complex aggregations.
4. **Audit Columns:** Inject comprehensive lineage and metadata columns (e.g., `silver_updated_at`, `invocation_id`).

*Note: Unlike the Staging layer, the Silver layer actively filters or flags bad data. Only high-quality, validated data is permitted to advance to the Gold layer or be captured by Snapshots.*

---

## 🥇 3. Gold Layer (Business & Reporting)
The Gold layer serves as the presentation zone. Data here is highly refined, aggregated, and business-ready. It powers our BI dashboards, ad-hoc analytics, and machine learning models. 
* **Snapshots (SCD Type 2)** are also built upon qualified data from this layer to track historical changes over time (e.g., corporate structural changes, dividend history).
